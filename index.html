"Where there is love and passion

there is power and magic."


Joseph Calabrese

I’m more comfortable leaving an analytical discussion of my artwork to others who can observe my work more objectively than I can, or wish to. Nonetheless – I will try telling you something of my work. Hopefully, in doing so – I may reveal to you something of myself.

My artwork materializes as it does. I don’t mean to suggest I don’t work hard -  or struggle to get it right. I do. But it is as natural apart of me as is my breathing. My artwork is a celebration of my life. It is the whole of my spiritual world. I do believe some things are best left unanswered. To analyze them is to compromise them – perhaps then – they lose their power and magic. Of course, the work begins with an idea – a feeling wanting to be expressed – translated into something visual – tangible. Conjuring up visual images which I then allow to take me where they will. I try not to get in the way. At some point there’s a bonding between the subject matter, the materials in which I’m working and myself. The process then becomes very fluid -  organic. Upon completion, if I feel the work has power and magic – I then consider myself as having been a good listener.

As for what I’m wanting to accomplish with my work? – Simple. To share my appreciation of Nature’s beauty. To bring much needed attention to some of its most threatened inhabitants. Or perhaps -  simply to share the beauty of a fallen Maple leaf. On a more personal level – the endless fascination and joy of bringing something into our world – that would otherwise not exist.
